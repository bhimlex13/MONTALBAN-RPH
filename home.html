Tourist Sites in Montalban

Mt. Lubog

  • Rodriguez, Rizal
  • Culture , Hiking Sites

Mt. Lubog offers an exciting day hike in Rizal that takes approximately 1 hour and 45 minutes, depending on your walking speed, to reach the summit. Similar to Mt. Lagyo and other mountains in Montalban, Mt. Lubog features limestone formations. What makes Mt. Lubog distinct from other hiking spots in Rodriguez is the dense forest cover that shelters climbers until they reach the open area at the rocky summit.

Standing at 955 meters above sea level (MASL), the summit provides a picturesque landscape of rocks, making hikers feel as though they are perched at one of the entrances to the stunning view of the southern Sierra Madre. The summit, often called "Simbahang Bato," resembles a maze of boulders and karst formations, adding to the allure of Mt. Lubog.

Avilon Zoo

  • Rodriguez, Rizal
  • Leisure , Wildlife Experience

Avilon Zoo is the largest place for animals in the Philippines. It's in Rodriguez, Rizal, and it covers 7.5 hectares. The zoo is home to more than 3,000 animals, including birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians, fishes, and invertebrates. They represent over 500 species! Many animals, especially those native to the Philippines, were born at the zoo through special programs that take care of them.

At Avilon Zoo, you can take guided tours and trips to learn about animals, their lives, and the efforts to save them. There are interesting animals to see, and you can also try feeding creatures like Arapaimas. The zoo has friendly animals that you can meet and have fun with!

Mt. Mal-ac

  • Rodriguez, Rizal
  • Culture , Hiking Sites

If you're seeking a less-traveled mountain hike with a refreshing waterfall stop, Mount Malac is a great choice. The trail mixes grassy ridges and a bit of rocky river trekking. While it takes about 2-3 hours to reach the summit, 1.5 hours to the waterfalls, and 2 hours back, totaling 5.5 to 6.5 hours of walking on sometimes uncovered paths, it's doable for determined beginners. Mount Malac is located southeast of Mount Cabuan and north of Mount Caypipili.

The trail starts in a dry riverbed before the climb. The first part has vegetation for shade, but some areas may show burned grass due to kaingin. As you get closer to the summit, grassland dominates, offering a panoramic view with a few scattered trees. A popular combo is to hike Mt. Macareba + Mt. Mal-ac with a side trip to Puray Falls or Baawan Waterfalls. Hiking Mount Mal-ac rewards with a stunning 360 view of the Sierra Madre Mountain, and you can even choose to camp overnight at Mogul Peak.

Palms and Terraces Swimming Pools

  • Brgy Chico St, Rodriguez, Rizal
  • Leisure, Recreation

The Palms and Terraces Swimming Pool in Montalban, Rizal, is more than just a swimming destination; it's a versatile venue that caters to various needs. As a public swimming pool, it offers a welcoming space for individuals and families to enjoy a leisurely swim in its well-maintained waters. Beyond being a recreational spot, this facility transforms into a hub for sports enthusiasts, serving as a Swimming Sports Facility where competitions and events can be hosted.

In addition to its sporting aspect, The Palms and Terraces Swimming Pool doubles as a charming Events Place, making it an ideal choice for special occasions like weddings, birthdays, and parties. The picturesque surroundings, combined with the well-designed pool area, create a vibrant atmosphere for celebrations. Whether you're looking for a place to swim, compete, or commemorate significant moments, this versatile public swimming pool has something to offer for everyone.

Mt. Sipit Ulang

  • Rodriguez, Rizal
  • Culture , Hiking Sites

In Rodriguez, Rizal, you'll find Mt. Sipit-Ulang, a popular spot known for its awesome rock formations and limestone paths. It's become a favorite destination lately, with a 3/9 difficulty grade and just a short walk up to 252 meters above sea level.

The name "Sipit Ulang" comes from a giant crab's petrified claw, seemingly reaching for the sky. This spot provides a fantastic view of Mt. Pamitinan, Mt. Binacayan, and Mt. Ayaas from its summit. The hike starts in a meadow after crossing a small brook, gradually going up through trees and plants, with some intimidating and eerie rocks and narrow formations. Navigating tight spaces and climbing sharp rocks can be a challenge, but the thrill of slipping and sliding through these paths adds to the adventure. The diverse rock formations along the trail enhance the beauty of the hike.

Wawa Dam

  • Rodriguez, Rizal
  • Environmental Sites, Nature preserve

Wawa Dam, also known as Montalban Dam, is a captivating tourist destination situated in the picturesque Montalban Gorge, part of the Sierra Madre Mountains in Rodriguez, Rizal, Philippines. This gravity dam, with its slight arch, stands proudly over the Marikina River, offering a stunning 360-meter high backdrop of the Montalban Gorge, creating a breathtaking water gap in the Sierra Madre.

Constructed in 1904 during the American colonial era, Wawa Dam started operating in 1909 to meet the water needs of Manila. Steeped in history, the dam played a crucial role in World War II as the battleground between the US Army and the Imperial Japanese Army during the Battle of Wawa Dam. Although no longer Manila's primary water source since the completion of Angat Dam in 1968, Wawa Dam has transformed into a captivating tourist site. Visitors can explore the historic dam, marvel at the surrounding landscape, and appreciate the protected natural beauty of the Pamitinan Protected Landscape. Wawa Dam stands as both a historical landmark and a serene haven for nature lovers, offering a glimpse into the past and a peaceful retreat in the present.

Heritage

Montalban, also called Rodriguez, is a place in Rizal, Philippines, with a strong history and culture. Its heritage shows a mix of native traditions, Spanish influence, and modern Filipino culture, highlighting the town's enduring spirit.

Montalban Festival

The Montalban Festival is a lively and culturally diverse celebration held each year in Rodriguez, Rizal, Philippines. It showcases the town's strong connection to its heritage, creative talents, and unity within the community. With vibrant festivities, it's a must-see for both locals and tourists, offering a unique and engaging experience.
